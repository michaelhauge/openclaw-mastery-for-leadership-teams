# 18. Review & Referral Solicitation

**Funnel Stage**: RETENTION
**Time Saved**: 3-5 hours per week
**Tools**: Claude + WhatsApp / Email
**Difficulty**: Beginner

---

## What This Workflow Does

Most businesses know they should ask for reviews and referrals — but they never do it systematically because writing personalised requests feels awkward and time-consuming. This workflow uses AI to draft personalised, conversational review requests and referral program messages that match your brand voice and reference specific wins from each customer's journey. The result is messages your customers actually respond to rather than ignore.

## The Prompt Template

### Template A: Google Review Request (WhatsApp)

```
You are a customer success specialist at a Malaysian SME. Write a WhatsApp message asking a happy customer to leave us a Google review.

MY BUSINESS:
- Business name: [YOUR BUSINESS NAME]
- What we do: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
- Google Maps / Google Business link: [YOUR GOOGLE REVIEW LINK]
- Our tone: [e.g., Professional but warm / Casual and friendly / Formal]

CUSTOMER CONTEXT:
- Customer name: [FIRST NAME]
- What they bought: [PRODUCT/SERVICE]
- Key win or result they experienced: [e.g., "saved 3 hours per week on payroll", "their product launch went smoothly", "their team loved the training session"]
- How long they've been a customer: [DURATION]
- Language preference: [Bahasa Malaysia / English / Mixed BM-English / Mandarin]

REQUIREMENTS:
- Keep it under 100 words
- Sound human and personal, not like a template
- Reference their specific result or win
- Include the Google review link naturally
- Do NOT sound like you're begging
- Add a simple P.S. if appropriate
- If language is BM or mixed, use appropriate Bahasa Malaysia phrases

Write 3 versions: one formal, one warm/casual, one in mixed BM-English (rojak style).
```

### Template B: Referral Program Message

```
You are a copywriter helping a Malaysian business owner ask happy customers to refer new clients.

MY BUSINESS:
- Business name: [YOUR BUSINESS NAME]
- What we sell: [PRODUCT/SERVICE]
- Referral incentive: [e.g., "RM 200 cash", "one free month", "10% off next invoice", "no incentive — just asking"]
- Referral process: [e.g., "they just WhatsApp me and mention your name", "fill in this form: [LINK]"]

CUSTOMER CONTEXT:
- Customer name: [FIRST NAME]
- Result they got from us: [SPECIFIC WIN]
- Who they might know: [e.g., "other HR managers", "F&B business owners", "logistics companies"]
- Relationship warmth: [Close/Medium/Formal]

WRITE:
1. A WhatsApp message asking for a referral (casual, under 120 words)
2. An email version (slightly longer, more structured, 150-200 words)
3. A LinkedIn message version (professional, 80-100 words)

For each version:
- Mention their specific result to remind them of the value they received
- Make the ask feel natural, not transactional
- Be specific about what kind of person/company they should refer
- Make the referral action as easy as possible (one step)
- Respect their time — do not over-explain the program
```

### Template C: Post-Project Testimonial Request

```
I need to ask a customer for a written testimonial I can use on my website and LinkedIn.

MY BUSINESS: [BUSINESS NAME] — [WHAT YOU DO]

CUSTOMER:
- Name: [NAME], [JOB TITLE] at [COMPANY]
- Project we worked on: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION]
- Key outcome: [RESULT IN NUMBERS IF POSSIBLE]
- Their personality: [e.g., Formal/Analytical / Warm/Expressive / Busy/Direct]

Write:
1. A WhatsApp request message (casual, under 80 words)
2. An email with 3 suggested testimonial prompts they can answer to make it easy
3. If they're too busy to write, write a DRAFT testimonial I can send for their approval (first person, in their voice, based on the outcome data above)

The testimonial should be 50-80 words, specific, credible, and not sound like marketing fluff.
```

## How to Use It

1. Identify customers who recently had a win, completed a project, or hit a positive milestone. These are your best review targets — the emotional high is still fresh.
2. Choose the right template (Google review, referral, or testimonial) and fill in the customer context fields.
3. Pick the version Claude writes that best matches your relationship with that customer. Edit any details that don't feel right, then send directly via WhatsApp or email.
4. For Google reviews: always include the short link (get it from your Google Business profile under "Share review form").

## Malaysia-Specific Tips

- **WhatsApp outperforms email for review requests in Malaysia by a wide margin.** Open rates on WhatsApp are near 90%. Most Malaysian SME clients will see and respond to a WhatsApp message within hours; an email might sit for days.
- **The best time to send review requests in Malaysia:** Tuesday-Thursday between 10am-12pm or 2-4pm. Avoid Fridays after Zohor prayer time.
- **Referrals flow through community networks.** Malaysian business owners refer within their industry associations (Dewan Perniagaan, NCCIM, EO chapters), Rakan Niaga groups, and WhatsApp group chats. Ask specifically: "Do you know anyone in your industry association who might benefit?"
- **For Malay-Muslim customers:** Avoid sending messages during Ramadan fasting hours (early afternoon). Festive periods (Hari Raya week) are generally too busy for business asks — wait a week after Raya for the right moment.
- **Mandarin-speaking business owners:** Consider asking Claude to write a Mandarin version if you serve Chinese-owned SMEs. A message in their language signals respect and significantly improves response rates.
- **Google reviews vs. Facebook reviews:** For B2C businesses in Malaysia, Facebook reviews and ratings are often more visible to local customers than Google. Ask Claude to write a Facebook review request as well.
