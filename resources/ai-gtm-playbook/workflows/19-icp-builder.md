# 19. ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) Builder

**Funnel Stage**: Intelligence
**Time Saved**: 10–20 hours (vs hiring a consultant at RM 10,000–20,000)
**Tools**: Claude / ChatGPT
**Difficulty**: Beginner

---

## What This Workflow Does

Feed AI your 3 best clients and it builds a detailed Ideal Customer Profile — including firmographics, psychographics, buying triggers, red flags, and lookalike targeting criteria. This is the foundation of all other workflows: get this right and every outreach effort becomes 3x more effective.

---

## The Prompt Template

```
You are a B2B go-to-market strategist specialising in the Malaysian mid-market.

I'm going to describe my 3 best clients. Analyse them and produce a detailed Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) for my business.

My business:
- Company name: [YOUR COMPANY]
- What we sell: [PRODUCT/SERVICE]
- Price point: [APPROXIMATE DEAL SIZE or MONTHLY RETAINER]

My 3 best clients:

Client 1:
- Company name: [NAME]
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- Company size: [EMPLOYEES / REVENUE]
- Location: [KL / Penang / Johor / etc.]
- Job title of main contact: [TITLE]
- Why they bought from us: [REASON]
- How long it took to close: [SALES CYCLE]
- Their main problem we solved: [PROBLEM]

Client 2:
[Same fields]

Client 3:
[Same fields]

Based on this, produce:

1. **ICP Summary** — 3-sentence description of my ideal customer
2. **Firmographics** — Industry, company size, revenue range, location, ownership type (local/MNC/GLC)
3. **Psychographics** — Mindset, priorities, fears, what they read, what they care about
4. **Buying Triggers** — What event or situation makes them ready to buy RIGHT NOW
5. **Red Flags** — Signs this prospect will NOT convert (save me time disqualifying)
6. **Decision-Maker Profile** — Job title, seniority, what they care about, how they make decisions
7. **Messaging That Works** — 3 value propositions that would resonate with this ICP
8. **Where to Find Them** — LinkedIn search parameters, associations, events, publications they read
9. **Lookalike Companies** — 5 specific company names in Malaysia that match this profile
10. **ICP Score Checklist** — 8 yes/no questions I can use to score any new prospect in 60 seconds
```

---

## How to Use It

1. Open Claude (claude.ai) — this works best with Claude Sonnet or Opus
2. Fill in all three client descriptions honestly — be specific, not generic
3. Paste the full prompt and run it
4. Review the output — push back on anything that doesn't feel right
5. Save the ICP document and share it with anyone involved in sales

## Example Input

```
My business: [YOUR COMPANY NAME] — [what you sell and price range]

Client 1:
- Takaful Malaysia (insurance, 800 employees, KLCC)
- Contact: Head of HR
- Why they bought: HRD Corp levy balance, leadership team wanted AI literacy
- Sales cycle: 3 weeks
- Problem: Leadership didn't know how to use AI tools they'd already subscribed to

Client 2:
- Sunway Group (property/education/healthcare conglomerate, 16,000 employees, Bandar Sunway)
- Contact: Group CHRO
- Why they bought: Board directive to assess AI readiness across business units
- Sales cycle: 6 weeks
- Problem: No consistent AI capability across senior leadership

Client 3:
- RHB Bank (banking, 14,000 employees, KL)
- Contact: Head of L&D
- Why they bought: BNM expectations on digital capability + unused HRD Corp levy
- Sales cycle: 4 weeks
- Problem: Compliance and productivity pressure from regulator
```

---

## Example Output (abbreviated)

**ICP Summary**: Mid-to-large Malaysian companies (500–15,000 staff) in regulated industries with a Head of HR or L&D as primary contact. Triggered to buy when leadership faces regulatory pressure, board mandates, or unused HRD Corp levy approaching the fiscal deadline.

**Top Buying Trigger**: HRD Corp levy balance above RM 50,000 + a board or regulatory directive requiring AI literacy across leadership.

**Red Flags**: Company under 100 employees (levy too small), pure startup culture (no L&D budget), recent retrenchment (training budgets frozen).

---

## Malaysia-Specific Tips

- **HRD Corp** is a powerful qualifier in Malaysia — companies with 10+ Malaysian staff pay a mandatory 1% payroll levy they must spend on training
- **GLCs and government-linked companies** have longer sales cycles but larger budgets and higher loyalty once won
- **Bumiputera ownership** unlocks different procurement processes — worth asking early
- The best ICP in Malaysia often comes from the intersection of: industry pressure + HRD Corp funding + leadership mandate
