# 11. Proposal & Pitch Deck Generator

**Funnel Stage**: Conversion
**Time Saved**: 4–6 hours per proposal
**Tools**: Claude + Gamma / Google Slides
**Difficulty**: Beginner

---

## What This Workflow Does

Generates a complete client proposal or pitch deck from a 2-sentence brief. Input what the client needs and what you offer — AI produces a structured, professional document ready to customise and send. Cuts proposal time from half a day to under 30 minutes.

---

## The Prompt Template — Written Proposal

```
You are a senior B2B proposal writer specialising in Malaysian corporate clients.

Write a client proposal for:

My company: [YOUR COMPANY]
Client company: [CLIENT NAME]
Client contact: [NAME, TITLE]
What they need: [THEIR PROBLEM / PROJECT BRIEF in 2–3 sentences]
What we're proposing: [YOUR SOLUTION — be specific]
Proposed investment: [PRICING — range or fixed]
Timeline: [PROJECT DURATION / START DATE]
Why us: [2–3 differentiators vs alternatives]
Social proof: [1–2 relevant past clients or outcomes]

Write a professional proposal with these sections:
1. Executive Summary (3–4 sentences — what we're proposing and why it matters)
2. Understanding Your Situation (shows we listened — summarise their challenge)
3. Our Proposed Solution (specific deliverables, not generic capabilities)
4. Implementation Timeline (week-by-week or phase-by-phase)
5. Investment (pricing table — be specific and transparent)
6. Why [YOUR COMPANY NAME] / Why Us (differentiation without arrogance)
7. Next Steps (clear CTA — what happens after they say yes)
8. Terms (1–2 key commercial terms — payment schedule, IP ownership if relevant)

Tone: Professional, confident, client-focused. This is about them, not us.
Format: Clean headings, short paragraphs. No buzzwords.
```

---

## The Prompt Template — Pitch Deck Outline

```
Create a 10-slide pitch deck outline for the same proposal above.

For each slide:
- Slide title
- 3–5 bullet points of content
- Suggested visual (chart / photo / diagram / screenshot)

The deck should flow as: Problem → Solution → How it works → Proof → Investment → Next Steps
```

## Example Input

```
My company: [YOUR COMPANY NAME]
Client: Affin Bank Malaysia
Contact: Datin Rozana, Chief Human Resources Officer
What they need: AI literacy programme for 80 senior managers across Affin Bank branches — board has mandated AI capability by end of Q2 2026
What we're proposing: 2-day AI for Leadership Teams workshop (tool-agnostic), SBL-Khas eligible, with 3-month follow-up check-in
Pricing: RM 48,000 for full programme (SBL-Khas claimable)
Timeline: Workshop in May 2026, check-in in August 2026
Why us: Malaysian market focus, HRD Corp expertise, worked with CIMB and RHB
Social proof: CIMB (300 leaders trained, 94% satisfaction), RHB (L&D team rated 9.2/10)
```

---

## Malaysia-Specific Tips

- **HRD Corp section**: Always include a dedicated section on SBL-Khas eligibility and how to claim — this removes a major procurement objection
- **Formal titles**: Address client contacts formally in the proposal (Dato', Datin, Dr., Tuan, Puan) if they carry those titles
- **Payment terms**: Malaysian corporates often prefer 30/70 or 50/50 payment splits — offer this proactively
- **Bahasa Malaysia**: Some GLCs and government clients prefer BM proposals — ask before submitting
