# 02. Short-Form Video Script Generator

**Funnel Stage**: AWARENESS
**Time Saved**: 2–4 hours per video (ideation + scripting + caption writing)
**Tools**: Claude / ChatGPT + CapCut + Canva
**Difficulty**: Beginner

---

## What This Workflow Does

Generates complete TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts scripts optimised for Malaysian audiences — including a scroll-stopping hook, timed shot-by-shot breakdown, spoken script, on-screen text, B-roll suggestions, and caption with hashtags. Removes the biggest blocker for most business owners: not knowing what to say or how to structure a 30–60 second video.

---

## The Prompt Template

```
You are a short-form video strategist who specialises in creating viral content for Malaysian business owners on TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts.

**BUSINESS CONTEXT**
Business name: [YOUR BUSINESS NAME]
Industry: [e.g., property agent / restaurant owner / HR consultant / logistics company]
Target audience: [e.g., first-time home buyers in KL aged 28–40 / HR managers at SMEs / F&B business owners]
Platform: [TikTok / Instagram Reels / YouTube Shorts — pick ONE]
Video length target: [15 seconds / 30 seconds / 60 seconds]

**VIDEO BRIEF**
Topic or angle: [e.g., "3 mistakes people make when buying their first property in Malaysia" / "Why your staff turnover is actually a training problem" / "How we cut food waste by 40% at our kopitiam"]
Video goal: [e.g., grow followers / generate DMs / drive traffic to website / build trust]
Call to action at end: [e.g., "Follow for more property tips" / "DM me the word QUOTE for a free consultation" / "Link in bio"]

**TONE & STYLE**
Preferred style: [e.g., educational / entertaining / behind-the-scenes / storytelling / listicle]
Presenter: [e.g., founder speaking to camera / voiceover with B-roll / text-only with music]
Language: [English / Bahasa Malaysia / Manglish (mixed English-Malay casual) / Chinese (Mandarin)]

**AUDIENCE INSIGHT**
One specific pain point or desire of the target audience: [e.g., "They're scared of getting cheated when buying property" / "They feel like their staff don't care about the company"]

**DELIVERABLES**
Please produce ALL of the following:

1. **Hook options** (give me 3 alternative opening lines — the first 3 seconds are critical)
2. **Full timed script** — broken into sections with timestamps (e.g., 0:00–0:03, 0:03–0:15)
   - For each section: spoken words + on-screen text (if different) + visual/B-roll suggestion
3. **Caption** (150–300 characters for TikTok/Reels — conversational, not corporate)
4. **Hashtag set**: 5 niche hashtags + 5 broad hashtags relevant to Malaysian audience
5. **Thumbnail/cover frame suggestion** (what the still frame at 0:00 should show)
6. **Follow-up video idea** (so this becomes part of a series)
```

---

## How to Use It

1. Complete the brief above. The "hook" and "audience insight" fields are the most important — don't leave them generic.
2. Run the prompt in Claude or ChatGPT. If the hook options don't feel right, ask: "Give me 5 more hook options — make them more provocative / more curiosity-driven / more specific to [your city]."
3. Record the video using your phone. CapCut (free, widely used in Malaysia) is recommended for editing — it auto-generates captions from your spoken script.
4. Add on-screen text, music, and transitions in CapCut or Canva Video.
5. Post natively to each platform (do not share the TikTok watermarked version to Reels — it gets suppressed).

## Malaysia-Specific Tips

- **Manglish performs well**: Malaysian TikTok audiences respond warmly to the natural code-switching between English and Bahasa Malaysia. Don't force full English — it can feel stiff.
- **Language by platform**: TikTok Malaysia skews younger and is more receptive to Manglish/Malay. Instagram Reels in Malaysia tends to be slightly more English or Mandarin-heavy depending on niche. YouTube Shorts works across all.
- **Festive content spikes**: Plan video series around Hari Raya (March/April), Chinese New Year (Jan/Feb), Merdeka (August). Property, F&B, fashion, and gifting topics perform exceptionally well during these windows.
- **Local hashtags**: Always include Malay/Malaysian hashtags alongside English ones. `#tiktokmalaysia`, `#malaysiatiktok`, `#tipsmalaisia`, `#usahawanmalaisia` each have large followings.
- **CapCut availability**: CapCut is free and works well on Malaysian Android and iOS devices. It auto-generates subtitles in English and Malay — turn this on for every video, as many Malaysians watch without sound.
- **Sensitive topics**: Avoid political commentary or religious content unless it's your core brand. In Malaysia, both can generate disproportionate backlash.

## Expected Results

- **Time saved**: From 3–4 hours per video (ideation + scripting) to 20–40 minutes
- **Consistency uplift**: Most Malaysian SME accounts post 0–2 videos/month. This workflow enables 3–5/week
- **Quality vs manual**: Script quality is strong; hooks are often better than what a non-creative business owner would write alone
- **ROI estimate**: TikTok and Reels accounts with consistent posting (3–5x/week) typically see 500–5,000 followers in the first 90 days. For a service business, 10 qualified DMs/month from social video at RM 5,000 average deal value = RM 50,000 in pipeline. Zero ad spend required.
- **Compounding effect**: Videos continue getting views weeks and months after posting — especially TikTok, which has a longer content shelf life than Instagram.
