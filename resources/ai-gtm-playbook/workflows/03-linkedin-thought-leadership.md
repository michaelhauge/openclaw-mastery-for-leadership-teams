# 03. LinkedIn Thought Leadership Engine

**Funnel Stage**: Awareness
**Time Saved**: 3–5 hours per week
**Tools**: Claude / ChatGPT + LinkedIn native
**Difficulty**: Beginner

---

## What This Workflow Does

Generates founder and executive LinkedIn posts that build credibility with your ICP. Consistent posting positions you as the go-to expert in your space — so when prospects are ready to buy, they already trust you. Designed for Malaysian B2B audiences.

---

## The Prompt Template

```
You are a LinkedIn ghostwriter for a Malaysian B2B founder/executive.

Write a LinkedIn post for the following:

Author: [YOUR NAME]
Role: [YOUR TITLE at COMPANY]
Topic: [WHAT THIS POST IS ABOUT]
Target reader: [WHO SHOULD SEE AND ENGAGE WITH THIS]
Core message: [THE ONE THING YOU WANT THEM TO TAKE AWAY]
Personal story or observation: [OPTIONAL — a real thing that happened or you noticed]
CTA: [WHAT SHOULD READERS DO? Comment / DM / Read / Share]

Post requirements:
- Hook (first line): Must stop the scroll — use a surprising stat, contrarian opinion, or bold statement
- Length: 150–250 words (LinkedIn sweet spot for reach)
- Format: Short paragraphs (1–2 lines each). No bullet points — they reduce reach.
- Tone: Direct, confident, professional but human. Malaysian English acceptable.
- End with a question to drive comments
- No hashtag stuffing — 2–3 relevant hashtags maximum, at the end only
- Do NOT open with "I" — LinkedIn algorithm suppresses posts starting with "I"

Avoid: Corporate jargon, humble-bragging, vague statements like "AI is the future"
```

---

## How to Use It

1. Pick one topic per week that your ICP would genuinely find useful
2. Run the prompt — generate 2–3 variations, pick the strongest opening line
3. Post Tuesday–Thursday 8–10am KL time (highest B2B reach window)
4. Reply to every comment in the first 60 minutes — boosts algorithmic reach
5. Repurpose high-performing posts into articles or email content

---

## Example Input

```
Author: [YOUR NAME], [YOUR TITLE] at [YOUR COMPANY NAME]
Topic: Why most Malaysian companies are paying for AI tools their teams don't know how to use
Target reader: HR Managers and CEOs at mid-market Malaysian companies
Core message: Buying AI tools without training is like buying gym equipment you never use
Personal story: Met a CFO last week who had 200 Copilot licences and zero staff using them
CTA: Comment below if you've seen this in your organisation
```

---

## Example Output

81% of Malaysian employers say they can't find AI-skilled talent.

Meanwhile, the same companies are paying monthly subscriptions for AI tools their teams open once and never touch.

I met a CFO last week with 200 Microsoft Copilot licences. When I asked how many of his team used it regularly — his answer was "maybe five."

That's RM 400,000 a year in tools. For five people.

The problem isn't the tools. It's that nobody showed the other 195 people why it matters to their specific job.

AI adoption isn't a technology problem. It's a training problem.

The companies winning with AI right now aren't the ones with the best tools — they're the ones who taught their people how to use them.

Have you seen this pattern in your organisation? I'd be curious to know.

## Malaysia-Specific Tips

- **Post in English** for KL corporate audience — BM posts reach a different (often non-ICP) demographic
- **Mention Malaysia** explicitly — "Malaysian companies", "in KL", "SEA market" improves relevance signals
- **Tag sparingly** — Only tag someone if they're directly relevant and you have a relationship with them
- **EO members**: Post about lessons from your business — EO culture of sharing resonates widely
