# Workflow 15: Case Study Generator

**Funnel Stage:** Conversion
**Time Saved:** 3–5 hours per case study
**Tools:** Claude, Google Docs / Canva
**Difficulty:** Beginner

---

## What This Workflow Does

Transforms a quick conversation with a happy client into a polished, persuasive case study — in under 10 minutes. No more case studies sitting half-finished in a Google Doc for six months. AI does the writing; you do the approving. The output is a professional document you can use on your website, in proposals, and in sales calls.

---

## The Prompt Template

### Part 1 — Client Interview (use this in a call or send as a questionnaire)

```
[Send these questions to your client before running the prompt]

1. What was the main challenge or problem you were trying to solve before working with us?
2. Why did you choose us over alternatives?
3. What specifically did we do together? (Key activities or deliverables)
4. What results have you seen? (Be as specific as possible — numbers, percentages, time saved, revenue impact)
5. What surprised you most about working with us?
6. Would you recommend us to a colleague? How would you describe us to them?
```

### Part 2 — Case Study Generation

```
Write a professional B2B case study based on the following client information.

CLIENT:
Company: [COMPANY NAME]
Industry: [INDUSTRY]
Company size: [EMPLOYEES / REVENUE]
Client contact name and title: [NAME, TITLE] (will be quoted)

SITUATION:
Challenge they faced: [PASTE ANSWER FROM Q1]
Why they chose us: [PASTE ANSWER FROM Q2]

SOLUTION:
What we did: [PASTE ANSWER FROM Q3]

RESULTS:
Outcomes achieved: [PASTE ANSWER FROM Q4]
What surprised them: [PASTE ANSWER FROM Q5]

QUOTE:
Use this verbatim quote from the client: "[PASTE ANSWER FROM Q6]"

Write a case study with this structure:
1. HEADLINE — Results-focused, specific (e.g. "How [Company] Saved 40% on Training Costs with AI")
2. SNAPSHOT BOX — Client name, industry, size, challenge, outcome (3–4 bullet points)
3. THE SITUATION — 2–3 paragraphs describing the challenge and context
4. THE SOLUTION — 2–3 paragraphs describing what was done and why
5. THE RESULTS — Bullet points with specific, quantified outcomes
6. CLIENT QUOTE — Pull quote in a callout box
7. ABOUT US — 2-sentence description of [YOUR COMPANY]

Tone: Professional, third-person, editorial. Not a press release.
Length: 500–700 words.
```

---

## How to Use It

1. After a successful client engagement, send the 6 client interview questions (Part 1) via email or WhatsApp
2. Once you have the responses, paste them into Part 2 and run
3. Edit the output for accuracy — check all numbers, quotes, and claims against client's actual words
4. Send the draft to the client for approval before publishing
5. Publish on your website and use in proposals

**Pro tip:** Collect answers during your client's offboarding or quarterly review call — they're already reflecting on results, so the responses are richer.

## Example Output (excerpt)

**Headline:** How Unilever Malaysia Upskilled 120 Leaders on AI in 60 Days

**Snapshot:**
- **Client:** Unilever Malaysia
- **Industry:** FMCG / Consumer Goods
- **Challenge:** Leadership team had zero structured AI exposure despite company-wide digital push
- **Outcome:** 94% of participants rated training "immediately applicable"; HRD Corp levy fully utilised

**Client Quote:**
> "We had tried to run AI sessions internally and they always felt too technical for our business leaders. [Your Company] built something our people actually used the next day."
> — *Head of Human Resources, Unilever Malaysia*

---

## Malaysia-Specific Tips

- Always mention if training was HRD Corp / SBL-Khas claimable — this is a significant buying signal for Malaysian HR decision-makers reading the case study
- Name-drop the industry and KL location (e.g. "Bangsar South headquarters") — Malaysian buyers scan for familiar context
- If the client is a GLC or government-linked company, get explicit approval before publishing — they often have communications protocols
- Include a line about bilingual delivery if applicable — "Delivered in English with Bahasa Malaysia materials" is a genuine differentiator
