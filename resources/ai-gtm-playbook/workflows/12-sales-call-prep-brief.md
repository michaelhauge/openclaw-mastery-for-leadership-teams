# 12. Sales Call Prep Brief

**Funnel Stage**: Conversion
**Time Saved**: 1.5–2 hours per call (normally spent on manual LinkedIn + Google research)
**Tools**: Claude / ChatGPT + Perplexity (optional)
**Difficulty**: Beginner

---

## What This Workflow Does

Input a prospect's name, company, and the meeting context. AI generates a 1-page briefing in 60 seconds: company overview, decision-maker profile, likely pain points, 3 tailored opening questions, objection handling scripts, and competitive positioning. You walk into every meeting prepared — without the manual research.

---

## The Prompt Template

```
You are a senior B2B sales advisor preparing me for a sales meeting.

Meeting details:
- Prospect company: [COMPANY NAME]
- Prospect name and title: [NAME, TITLE]
- Meeting type: [Discovery call / Demo / Proposal review / Follow-up]
- My company: [YOUR COMPANY]
- What I'm selling: [PRODUCT/SERVICE — be specific]
- How I found this prospect: [Cold email / Referral / Inbound / Event]
- Anything I already know about them: [NOTES — even if empty]

Using publicly available knowledge about [COMPANY NAME] and the [INDUSTRY] industry in Malaysia, produce a 1-page Sales Call Prep Brief:

1. **Company Snapshot** (4–5 bullet points)
   - Industry and what they do
   - Approximate size (employees / revenue if known)
   - Recent news or developments relevant to my offer
   - Key business challenges in their sector right now

2. **Decision-Maker Profile**
   - What a [TITLE] typically cares about professionally
   - Their likely KPIs and what they're measured on
   - What a "win" looks like for them personally

3. **Likely Pain Points** (3 specific to their industry/role)
   - Pain point + why it matters to them + how my offer addresses it

4. **3 Opening Questions** (open-ended, not pitchy)
   - Questions that uncover budget, authority, need, and timeline
   - Questions that make the prospect feel understood, not interrogated

5. **Anticipated Objections + Responses** (top 3)
   - Objection → honest, non-defensive response
   - Include: budget, timing, incumbent vendor

6. **Competitive Landscape**
   - Who else they might be talking to
   - My differentiated positioning vs those alternatives

7. **Suggested Next Step**
   - What I should try to close at the end of this meeting
   - What a successful outcome looks like
```

---

## How to Use It

1. Open Claude (claude.ai) — 60 seconds before or the evening before your call
2. Fill in the template — even partial information gives good output
3. Review the brief — add any personal notes you know about this contact
4. Print or keep on a second screen during your call
5. After the call: run the Follow-Up Sequence workflow (Workflow 13)

---

## Example Input

```
Prospect company: CIMB Bank Malaysia
Prospect name and title: Farah Hanim, Head of Talent Development
Meeting type: Discovery call
My company: [YOUR COMPANY NAME]
What I'm selling: AI literacy workshop for CIMB's leadership team (50 senior managers), SBL-Khas eligible, RM 35,000 for full programme
How I found them: Cold email via Instantly campaign
What I know: Farah replied saying she's "exploring options for leadership development in Q2"
```

---

## Example Output (abbreviated)

**Company Snapshot**:
- CIMB is Malaysia's second-largest bank by assets; ~35,000 employees across SEA
- Heavy regulatory pressure from BNM on digital and AI capability
- Recently announced "Forward23+" transformation programme — AI capability is a stated pillar
- CIMB Group has an active HRD Corp account (large levy balance as a major employer)
- Recent news: CIMB launched internal AI tools in 2025 but adoption among senior leaders reportedly low

**Decision-Maker Profile (Head of Talent Development)**:
- Measured on: training completion rates, capability assessment scores, HRD Corp utilisation
- Cares about: programmes that are credible, practical, and easy to justify to CFO
- Personal win: delivering a high-profile leadership initiative that gets visible C-suite buy-in

**Top Opening Question**: "You mentioned exploring leadership development options for Q2 — what's driving the priority right now? Is this coming from the board, or more from what you're seeing on the ground with your leaders?"

---

## Malaysia-Specific Tips

- **Titles matter** — Dato', Datin, Tan Sri holders in Malaysian companies expect a higher level of preparedness
- **Dual hierarchy** — In GLCs and large Malaysian conglomerates, there's often a formal approver and an informal champion; figure out which one you're talking to
- **Relationship before transaction** — First meeting goal is often a second meeting, not a close
- **HRD Corp** — Always know if they're registered and approximate levy balance before the call
