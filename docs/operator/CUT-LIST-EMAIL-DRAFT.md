---

## Subject line options

Pick one — A is most direct, B is softest:

- **A.** `A change to tomorrow's OpenClaw workshop — please read`
- **B.** `Tomorrow's workshop: an honest update + your priority spot in our next cohort`
- **C.** `OpenClaw Mastery: I need to move you to our next cohort (sorry)`

I'd recommend **A** — it signals urgency without being clickbait, and "please read" is a real ask people respect.

## Why this email works (calibration notes for your own reference)

- **Owns the cause** ("I didn't catch in time") instead of blaming Hetzner. Cleanly signals competence over excuses. Anyone who'd respect you less for blaming a vendor will respect you more for owning the problem.
- **States the trade explicitly** ("rather than throw you into a half-working session"). Frames the cut as a quality decision, not a capacity failure.
- **Concrete recovery date** turns "you're cut" into "you're rescheduled". Different emotional response.
- **Three concrete value adds** (1:1 call, setup guide, captain brief) — converts the inconvenience into a perk. The 1:1 call is the heaviest weight because it scales with their seniority.
- **Easy out** ("can't make it → reply, I'll refund or reschedule") removes the trapped feeling.
- **Short closing** without over-apologizing — one "sorry" lands, three feel performative.
